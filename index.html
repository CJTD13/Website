Compression algorithm

I made a compression algorithm in C# using the arithmetic coding compression algorithm for a 1st year project in my physics degree. I also explored the LZW and Huffman coding algorithms, as well as the Wheeler-Burrows transform. Unfortunately, the final product was flawed (not lossless as intended) due to an issue with float underflow, resulting in varying levels of success in exactly reconstructing the original message.

Thermodynamic Simulation

In this project, coded in python with the SciPy and matplotlib modules, a gas was modelled as a collection of hard spheres undergoing elastic collisions. I tested several thermodynamics equations, including the relationship between the degrees of freedom of a 'molecule' and ratio of gas pressure to gas density. The project included a visual representation of the collisions to make debugging easier. As well as the collision snapshot version of the simulation (used for the calculations) , I also made a continuous motion simulation, containing up to 1500 hard spheres.
